What is a Trojan Horse?

A Trojan is malware that pretends to be legitimate software so a user will install it. Once on a device, it can open remote access, spy on activity, steal data, or pull in more malicious programs. Trojans usually arrive through phishing emails, deceptive downloads, or shady ads, and often rely on social engineering to get past a user’s guard. Source

Most Common Trojan Types

Trojan Type What it does
Backdoor Trojan Gives attackers remote control of the system and is often used to build botnets.
Banker Trojan Targets financial accounts and tries to steal banking and payment credentials.
DDoS Trojan Uses infected machines to flood a target with traffic and cause outages.
Downloader Trojan Fetches and installs additional malware after the first infection.
Exploit Trojan Abuses known software vulnerabilities to compromise systems.
